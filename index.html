About Us

Welcome to the Association of Mechatronics Engineering Students (AMTES) at FUNAAB! We’re about more than just academics; we’re here to build a thriving community within the Department of Mechatronics Engineering, a unique program under the College of Engineering in Funaab. Our association focuses on creating a vibrant environment where every Mechatronics student feels connected and supported.

We’re all about catering to the diverse needs of our students, providing ample opportunities for growth and development. It’s not just about lectures and labs; we bring you a variety of events, workshops, and all the exciting extras that make university life memorable!

With us, learning extends far beyond the classroom; every member gets the opportunity to be part of something bigger – creating, innovating, and shaping the future of engineering. We’ve got your back, offering a buffet of opportunities to ensure you’re well-equipped to rock the engineering world.

So, are you ready to explore endless possibilities and make the most of your time at FUNAAB? Join us!"

Fields in Mechatronics

ROBOTICS

Robotics involves designing and building autonomous machines for various tasks and applications.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Artificial intelligence involves developing algorithms and models that enable machines to perform tasks requiring human intelligence.

AUTOMOTIVE ENGINEERING

Automotive engineering focuses on the design, development, manufacturing, and testing of vehicles, incorporating elements of mechanical, electrical, electronic, software, and safety engineering.

SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

Software engineering is the application of engineering principles to software development, encompassing methodologies for design, coding, testing, and maintenance of software systems.

INDUSTRIAL AUTOMATION

Industrial automation employs control systems like computers or robots for handling different processes and machinery in manufacturing plants to enhance efficiency and reliability.

EMBEDDED SYSTEMS

Embedded systems are specialized computing devices that do not look like computers, designed to perform dedicated functions or tasks within larger systems.
